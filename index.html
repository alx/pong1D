// How to Play

01
🕹️ Stand at your end Each player takes a position at one end of the LED strip. Your button is right in front of you.
02
💡 Watch the light ball A white light travels along the strip from one player to the other — that's the ball.
03
👆 Press when it reaches you Hit your button exactly when the ball arrives at your end of the strip to return it.
04
⏱️ Timing is everything Press too early or too late and the opponent scores a point.
05
⚡ Power-up — super speed Press your button when the light is right next to it to trigger a speed bonus — the ball rockets to the other side!
06
🏆 First to 5 wins Score 5 points to win the match. The strip lights up in your color for a victory animation.

// About

Pong 1D reinvents the classic Pong game in a single dimension — a strip of LEDs replaces the screen. Players control a physical paddle along the strip, sending a light "ball" back and forth at increasing speed.

Built for events, hackathons, and maker fairs, Pong 1D supports 2 to 4 players simultaneously. The game is open-source and can be assembled with common components.
